Introduction

Unity In Bible Reading

This plan provides a consistent reading experience for reading the entire Bible in one year. Other canonical Bible reading plans are divided by chapters. Some chapters are very long, while others are very short. Dividing the reading this way will lead to inconsistencies in how much you read each day. We have designed a more consistent experience by dividing it by headings and chapters.

How It Was Made

Headings have been included as dividing points, providing a more consistent reading experience. Headings do not exist in the original books of the Bible but were added by English translators. A heading may exist in one translation but not another. In order to remove bias we selected the six most prominent English translations: CSB, ESV, NASB, NIV, NKJV, and NLT. For the New Testament, a heading was only chosen as a potential dividing point if it existed at the same verse for at least four of the six translations. Since the Old Testament (OT) has fewer headings per word count, and less agreement on the location of the headings, an OT heading was included if at least three of the six translations had a heading at that verse. After identifying the potential headings, it was time to select which ones would be in the plan. First, we calculated the average word count of every verse. We took the average word count of the entire Bible, divided it by the total number of days in the year (365), then rounded to the nearest heading and/or chapter. Depending on your translation, the reading might not end at a heading or chapter. Please keep in mind that it does end at a heading for other translations.

Why Canonical Order?

The Bible is a collection of books. Canonical order refers to the present-day order of those books. We believe, just as the books of the Bible are divinely inspired, so too is the order. Reading the Bible in its present order empowers us to learn in a way that is not possible when jumping between books. The books of the Bible differ in authorship, historical context, and literary style. It is easier to understand the context when reading through one book at a time.
